The Problem

Good Experiences Go Unasked For. Bad Ones Can Go Public First.

Most businesses deliver good care consistently — but never ask for reviews in a systematic way. The result is that a small number of unhappy experiences disproportionately shape the online reputation, while the happy majority goes unrecorded.

No consistent review requests

Reviews come in sporadically when patients decide to leave one. Most happy patients are never prompted, so they never bother.

Unhappy patients go public

Without a private feedback channel, a patient with a complaint goes straight to Google. The business finds out after the damage is done.

Referrals never asked for

Word-of-mouth is the highest-quality source of new patients — but most businesses never formally ask satisfied patients to refer others.

No rebooking prompts

Patients who have had a positive experience and are due for a follow-up appointment often need a gentle reminder. Without one, they drift away.

How private feedback routing works
When a patient responds with negative feedback, the system routes their message to the practice team privately — before they are directed to a public review platform. This gives your team the opportunity to address the issue before it appears publicly. This does not prevent patients from leaving public reviews if they choose to.
CMA and ASA compliance
Review solicitation must comply with UK Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations, CMA guidance, and ASA rules. We build all flows to be transparent and compliant — we never incentivise reviews or direct only happy patients to review platforms in a misleading way. All review requests are honest and optional.
What This Achieves

A Stronger Reputation That Attracts More Patients

Reviews and referrals are among the highest-converting sources of new patients. A consistent system for generating both — while protecting your reputation from public complaints — compounds over time into a significant competitive advantage.
